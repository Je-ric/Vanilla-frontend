About Us

Office of the Student Affairs (OSA)

The OSA serves as the center of information, activities, and services related to the co-curricular and extra-curricular needs of students. It also promotes the development of student's talents, potentials, and leadership capabilities through its program thrusts of self-growth and awareness, cooperative living and learning, leadership development and enhancement, productive use of leisure, and enhanced cross-cultural adjustment.

Services

Counseling Services

Mental Health Counseling: Individual counseling sessions where students can talk to trained professionals about their thoughts, feelings, and concerns.

Career counseling: Guides students in making informed decisions about academic and career paths, helping them explore interests, skills, and values while offering guidance on choosing majors, career options, and planning for post-graduation employment.

Personal Development Support: It ncompasses assisting students in developing interpersonal skills, time management, stress management, and other crucial life skills through workshops and programs, fostering personal growth and success in college and beyond.

Health Services

Healthcare: This can include general medical care for common illnesses, injuries, and preventive care.

Medical clinics: Provide a convenient and accessible resource on campus, offering a range of services such as routine check-ups, vaccinations, and treatment for minor illnesses or injuries to address students' healthcare needs.

Wellness Programs: Health services often extend beyond reactive care to include proactive wellness programs. These programs may focus on promoting healthy lifestyles, preventive healthcare measures, and educational initiatives.
